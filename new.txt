    <section id="about">
        <div class="container">
            <h2 class="section-title">About El Seibo Mission</h2>
            
            <h3 style="color: #2c5aa0; margin-top: 2rem;">Our History</h3>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                El Seibo Mission serves the communities in and around El Seibo, Dominican Republic, a region with a rich 
                but challenging history. The area has long been associated with the sugar industry, where many Haitian 
                migrants and their descendants have worked in the sugarcane fields under difficult conditions.
            </p>

            <h3 style="color: #2c5aa0; margin-top: 2rem;">The Bateys of El Seibo</h3>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                El Seibo province is home to many of the approximately 425 batey communities that exist across the Dominican Republic. These settlements house around 200,000 people total across the country, with significant concentrations in El Seibo and neighboring provinces. The word "batey" has roots in the Ta√≠no language, referring to a yard or open space, but today these communities represent some of the most impoverished settlements in the entire nation.
            </p>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                The bateys began forming in the early 20th century when large numbers of Haitians migrated to the Dominican Republic for seasonal sugarcane work. What were meant to be temporary housing for migrant workers during harvest season became permanent settlements. In the mid-1960s, the Dominican government formalized these as company-owned towns‚Äîcrude barracks surrounded by fencing and monitored by armed guards to prevent workers from leaving, essentially creating work camps.
            </p>

            <h3 style="color: #2c5aa0; margin-top: 2rem;">Daily Life in the Bateys</h3>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                Housing in the bateys consists of rudimentary structures made of wood or corrugated metal, often with slatted wood walls, tin roofs, and dirt floors. Many homes house eight or more people in cramped conditions. Bateys traditionally have no running water, electricity, or cooking facilities.
            </p>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                83% of the population of the bateys lacks access to drinking water. In communities like Batey Nueve, residents share water from a free water tank, but the water is not treated, leading to widespread ingestion of contaminated water. Children suffer from diarrhea and vomiting due to non-chlorinated water. The only bathrooms are often outside homes, shared with the entire community.
            </p>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                Workers spend 12 hours a day, six days a week in the sugarcane fields, cutting heavy stalks by the metric ton. During the heyday of the Dominican sugar economy in the 1960s through 1980s, daily wages were barely enough to buy one meal a day‚Äîoftentimes cane cutters and their families had nothing to eat but the very cane they cut. Today, some workers still earn barely enough to afford a single meal per day for their families.
            </p>

            <h3 style="color: #2c5aa0; margin-top: 2rem;">Health Crisis in the Bateys</h3>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                The health conditions in batey communities are among the worst in the Dominican Republic. Chronic malnutrition is highest in the bateys, and only 36% of children aged 18-29 months are fully vaccinated. HIV prevalence in the bateys stands at 3.2%, compared to the national level of 0.8%.
            </p>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                Families live in subpar conditions that compound the effects of contaminated drinking water and make it harder to maintain good hygiene. Water-borne diseases are normalized in these communities, with residents at constant risk of cholera, typhoid, and parasitic infections. Early pregnancy affects 34.9% of adolescents in the bateys, far above national averages.
            </p>

            <h3 style="color: #2c5aa0; margin-top: 2rem;">The Citizenship Crisis</h3>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                Perhaps the most devastating aspect of batey life is the documentation crisis. The Constitution of the Dominican Republic does not extend citizenship to children born to non-naturalized Haitian parents. This means that children born in the Dominican Republic to Haitian workers‚Äîsecond, third, and even fourth generation residents‚Äîhave no birth certificates or identity papers of any kind.
            </p>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                An overwhelming 85% of adolescents in the bateys lack civil status documentation and are therefore unable to access education, vocational training, employment opportunities, or healthcare offered outside of the bateys. This situation deepens the poverty and exclusion of these communities, making them easy targets for abuse and human rights violations.
            </p>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                The Dominican Republic has created a permanent underclass‚Äîa category of individuals that, in the eyes of the law, doesn't exist‚Äîthey have no right to own property, no right to an education, no access to healthcare, and no right to vote. These families are condemned to generational poverty with no legal pathway out.
            </p>

            <h3 style="color: #2c5aa0; margin-top: 2rem;">Geographic and Social Isolation</h3>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                Geographically and socially isolated, bateys lack basic healthcare, adequate water and sanitation, and access to education, while employment and income generation opportunities have grown increasingly scarce. Many bateys are located far from Dominican towns, creating physical barriers to accessing services even when documentation isn't an issue.
            </p>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                In the mid-1990s, when world markets switched from cane sugar to high-fructose corn syrup, the Dominican government was forced to privatize the sugar industry and close many struggling plantations. Without cane to cut, the already sparse economy of the bateys completely dried up. The armed guards and fences that once imprisoned workers disappeared, but families remained trapped by poverty, lack of documentation, and limited opportunities.
            </p>

            <h3 style="color: #2c5aa0; margin-top: 2rem;">Culture and Resilience</h3>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                Despite these overwhelming challenges, there is a strong sense of community and resilience in the bateys, where people rely on each other for support, sharing resources and celebrating together during festivals and special occasions. The bateys pulse with a unique Afro-Dominican culture, blending African traditions, Dominican customs, and Haitian heritage.
            </p>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                Gaga, a vibrant form of music and dance linked to Holy Week celebrations, is particularly prominent in bateys. Religious syncretism is common, blending Catholicism with African spiritual beliefs. Many residents speak both Spanish and Krey√≤l (Haitian Creole), maintaining connections to their Haitian heritage while navigating Dominican society.
            </p>

            <h3 style="color: #2c5aa0; margin-top: 2rem;">Our Mission</h3>
            <p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                El Seibo Mission was founded to address these critical healthcare needs. We provide medical services, health 
                education, and compassionate care to those who need it most. Our work focuses on:
            </p>
            <ul style="margin-left: 2rem; margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">
                <li>Delivering primary healthcare to underserved batey communities</li>
                <li>Providing preventive care and health education</li>
                <li>Offering medical services regardless of documentation status or ability to pay</li>
                <li>Building relationships and trust within the communities we serve</li>
                <li>Partnering with local organizations to create sustainable healthcare solutions</li>
            </ul>

            <h3 style="color: #2c5aa0; margin-top: 2rem;">Our Impact</h3>
            <p>
                Through regular medical mission trips and ongoing partnerships, we've been able to serve thousands of 
                patients, many of whom are Haitian migrants and their families who work or have worked in the sugarcane 
                industry. We believe every person deserves access to quality healthcare and the dignity of compassionate 
                medical treatment.
            </p>
        </div>

        function unlockGallery() {
            const password = document.getElementById('gallery-password').value;
            const alert = document.getElementById('login-alert');

            if (password === galleryPassword) {
                galleryUnlocked = true;
                document.getElementById('images-locked').style.display = 'none';
                document.getElementById('images-unlocked').style.display = 'block';
                alert.style.display = 'none';
            } else {
                alert.className = 'alert error';
                alert.textContent = 'Incorrect password. Please try again.';
                alert.style.display = 'block';
            }
        }

        function unlockGuestUpload() {
            const name = document.getElementById('guest-name').value.trim();
            const password = document.getElementById('guest-password').value;
            const alert = document.getElementById('guest-login-alert');

            if (!name) {
                alert.className = 'alert error';
                alert.textContent = 'Please enter your name.';
                alert.style.display = 'block';
                return;
            }

            if (password === guestPassword) {
                guestUnlocked = true;
                guestName = name;
                document.getElementById('guest-locked').style.display = 'none';
                document.getElementById('guest-unlocked').style.display = 'block';
                document.getElementById('guest-name-display').textContent = guestName;
                document.getElementById('guest-folder-name').textContent = guestName.replace(/\s+/g, '_');
                
                // Initialize guest folder if it doesn't exist
                if (!guestImages[guestName]) {
                    guestImages[guestName] = [];
                }
                
                alert.style.display = 'none';
            } else {
                alert.className = 'alert error';
                alert.textContent = 'Incorrect password. Please try again.';
                alert.style.display = 'block';
            }
        }

        function uploadImages() {
            const fileInput = document.getElementById('image-upload');
            const alert = document.getElementById('upload-alert');
            
            if (fileInput.files.length === 0) {
                alert.className = 'alert error';
                alert.textContent = 'Please select at least one image to upload.';
                alert.style.display = 'block';
                return;
            }

            const gallery = document.getElementById('gallery');
            
            Array.from(fileInput.files).forEach(file => {
                const reader = new FileReader();
                reader.onload = function(e) {
                    const galleryItem = document.createElement('div');
                    galleryItem.className = 'gallery-item';
                    galleryItem.innerHTML = `<img src="${e.target.result}" alt="Uploaded image">`;
                    gallery.appendChild(galleryItem);
                    uploadedImages.push(e.target.result);
                };
                reader.readAsDataURL(file);
            });

            alert.className = 'alert success';
            alert.textContent = `Successfully uploaded ${fileInput.files.length} image(s)!`;
            alert.style.display = 'block';
            fileInput.value = '';

            setTimeout(() => {
                alert.style.display = 'none';
            }, 3000);
        }

        function uploadGuestImages() {
            const fileInput = document.getElementById('guest-image-upload');
            const alert = document.getElementById('guest-upload-alert');
            
            if (fileInput.files.length === 0) {
                alert.className = 'alert error';
                alert.textContent = 'Please select at least one image to upload.';
                alert.style.display = 'block';
                return;
            }

            const gallery = document.getElementById('guest-gallery');
            
            // Clear the "no photos" message if it exists
            if (gallery.querySelector('p')) {
                gallery.innerHTML = '';
            }
            
            Array.from(fileInput.files).forEach(file => {
                const reader = new FileReader();
                reader.onload = function(e) {
                    const galleryItem = document.createElement('div');
                    galleryItem.className = 'gallery-item';
                    galleryItem.innerHTML = `<img src="${e.target.result}" alt="Photo by ${guestName}">`;
                    gallery.appendChild(galleryItem);
                    
                    // Store in guest's folder
                    guestImages[guestName].push({
                        data: e.target.result,
                        filename: file.name,
                        uploadDate: new Date().toISOString()
                    });
                };
                reader.readAsDataURL(file);
            });

            alert.className = 'alert success';
            alert.textContent = `Successfully uploaded ${fileInput.files.length} image(s) to your folder: ${guestName.replace(/\s+/g, '_')}`;
            alert.style.display = 'block';
            fileInput.value = '';

            setTimeout(() => {
                alert.style.display = 'none';
            }, 3000);
        }
